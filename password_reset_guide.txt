ARTICLE: How to Reset Your Forgotten Password

If you cannot remember your password, follow these steps to regain access to your account:

1. Go to the login page and click the link labeled "Forgot Password?" located directly below the password field.
2. Enter the email address associated with your account.
3. Check your inbox for an email titled "Password Reset Request." This email typically arrives within 2-5 minutes. If it does not appear, check your Spam or Junk folder.
4. Click the "Reset Password" button inside that email. This link is valid for 30 minutes only. If the link has expired, simply repeat Step 1 to receive a new one.
5. You will be taken to a secure page where you can enter a new password. Your new password must be at least 8 characters long and contain one number and one special character (such as ! or @).
6. After submitting, you will be logged in automatically and redirected to your dashboard.

If you do not receive the reset email after 10 minutes, please verify that you are using the correct email address. Accounts created using a "Sign in with Google" or "Sign in with Apple" method do not have a traditional password and cannot be reset this way; in that case, use the original sign-in provider's login screen instead.

ARTICLE: Account Locked After Multiple Failed Login Attempts

For security purposes, our system temporarily locks an account after 5 consecutive failed login attempts within a 15-minute window. This is an automated safety measure to protect your account from unauthorized access attempts.

What happens during a lockout:
- The account remains locked for 30 minutes from the time of the 5th failed attempt.
- You will see the message: "Too many login attempts. Please try again later."
- No data is lost or changed during a lockout; this only restricts login access temporarily.

How to resolve it:
- Option A: Simply wait 30 minutes and attempt to log in again with the correct password.
- Option B: Use the "Forgot Password?" link to reset your password immediately, which also clears the lockout instantly.
- Option C: If you believe the lockout was triggered by someone else attempting unauthorized access to your account, please change your password immediately after regaining access and enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) under Account Settings > Security.

ARTICLE: Setting Up Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Two-Factor Authentication adds an extra layer of security to your account by requiring a second verification step beyond just your password.

To enable 2FA:
1. Log in to your account and navigate to Account Settings.
2. Click on the "Security" tab.
3. Select "Enable Two-Factor Authentication."
4. Choose your preferred method: Authenticator App (such as Google Authenticator or Authy) or SMS Text Message.
5. If using an Authenticator App, scan the QR code shown on screen using your chosen app. Enter the 6-digit code generated by the app to confirm setup.
6. If using SMS, enter your phone number. You will receive a text message containing a 6-digit verification code. Enter this code to confirm setup.
7. Once confirmed, you will be shown a set of 10 backup codes. Save these in a secure location; each backup code can be used once if you lose access to your authentication method.

If you lose access to both your authenticator app and your backup codes, please contact support directly for manual identity verification, as this process can take 24-48 hours for security reasons.

ARTICLE: Changing Your Registered Email Address

To change the email address linked to your account:

1. Log in using your current credentials.
2. Go to Account Settings > Profile.
3. Click "Change Email Address."
4. Enter your new desired email address and your current account password to confirm your identity.
5. A confirmation link will be sent to the NEW email address. You must click this link within 24 hours to complete the change.
6. Until the confirmation link is clicked, your account will continue using the old email address for login purposes.

Please note: for security reasons, email address changes cannot be processed if your account was created less than 7 days ago, or if you have changed your email address within the last 30 days already.
